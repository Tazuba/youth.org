ORGANIZER :Trident Youth Engagement / Digital Literacy Initiative

COUNTRY: Uganda
LOCATION: Uganda Institute of Information and Communication Technology, Nakawa Uganda
PARTICIPANTS: 100
DATE : 27th & 28th November /2019

Set-up of the meeting: livestream, zoom participation

Over 100 participants attended and took action on the various issues discussed in the Internet Governance Forum in Berlin Germany. 2019. Day 1 (27/11/19) was more of remote view as we were streaming live using YouTube and Day 2 was the more engaging day as we used Zoom to participate in the sessions via online participation. Day 1, we streamed closing the Digital Gap for Marginalized Communities and Youth in IG for Internet Ethics & Digital Inclusion during the morning and early afternoon. Later, we streamed Community Networks: Opportunities, Challenges and Solutions and called it a day. These sessions were of great impact and we are trying to lobby for a camera to do video recording of the student expressing what they learnt from the sessions. Day 2 (28/11/19) we streamed Emerging technologies and their interfaces with inclusion, security and human rights (NRIs Perspectives) and IPv6: Why should I Care? In the early afternoon. In the evening, we streamed Digitally Skilling our Youth: Varied Global Approaches and Unlocking the Digital Potential of the DLDC Countries. These sessions, the participants engaged and we proud of them as some it was their first encounter.

RECOMMENDATIONS:

  • For the next events, breaks together with lunch should be provided to the participants to ensure continued participation and commitment.
  • Massive advertising is required for larger turn ups of participants in the various sessions.
  • Transport reimbursement should be provided to supporting staff.
  • More days of engagement should be considered to have more inputs from the participating group.
